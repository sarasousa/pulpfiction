Vincent Vega

Played by: John Travolta

Whether shooting up or shooting perps, Vincent remains the picture of coolness, even when he's on brain detail or administering a shot of adrenaline to a prone Mia's chest.

Jules Winnfield

Played by: Samuel L. Jackson

Jules Winnfield is the coolest cat of all. A Bible-spouting, burger-eating, jheri-curled hitman, every syllable the man utters is quotable and every look he gives could stop you in your tracks.

Mia Wallace

Played by: Uma Thurman

With a wit only as sharp as her bob, Mrs Mia Wallace is a very modern gangster's moll: a livewire thrill-seeker whose actions are even more irresponsible than her husband's. If you ignore the overdose, she's a delightful dinner companion, able to alleviate any awkward silences and capable of tearing up the dance floor. Just remember: look but don't touch.

Butch Coolidge

Played by: Bruce Willis

“In the fifth, your ass goes down.” Except boxer Butch ain't nobody's bitch. Fleeing from the scene after accidentally killing his opponent in the ring, Coolidge is still the closest thing Pulp Fiction has to a good guy. Butch later embraces his bloodlust by slicing and dicing his would-be rapists, who couldn't have picked a worse guy to screw with.

Marsellus Wallace

Played by: Ving Rhames

Marsellus Wallace is a gang boss and husband to Mia Wallace. He is the boss of Vincent Vega, Jules Winnfield, Butch Coolidge, and many other unknown gangsters. He is said to have thrown a man off a building for giving Mia a foot massage, and he is a victim of rape, courtesy of Zed.

"The Wolf"

Played by: Harvey Keitel

"I'm Winston Wolf. I solve problems.” The Wolf is a man capable of un-screwing any situation, lickety split. He also dresses like he's en route to his own wedding.

"Pumpkin" and "Honey Bunny" (aka Ringo and Yolanda)

Played by: Tim Roth and Amanda Plummer

Opening Pulp Fiction with a lengthy diatribe about the relative safety of robbing banks over liquor stores, petty thieves Pumpkin and Honey Bunny eventually kick off the action by pulling out their guns and yelling: “Everybody be cool!”.

Fabienne

Played by: Maria de Medeiros

Fabienne is the french girl fascinated with perfectly round pot-bellies. She's also the Butch's girlfriend.

Lance

Played by: Eric Stoltz

Lance is Vincent Vega's Drug dealer. He also saves the life of Mia Wallace, technically.

Captain Koons

Played by: Christopher Walken

Koons, a decorated Vietnam vet, tells little Butch the story of how he and the young man's father hid a watch up their asses for seven years.
